Critical score scale

  1. Dangerous or scary performance. The driver has run out of patience with it. The system does not work. Must be improved.
  2. The driver feels uncomfortable or dissatisfied. The system does not work correctly, but it is not critical. Customer complaints are possible.
  3. Neutral. The system may perform as expected or incorrectly, but it is indifferent for the driver and drivability. Customer complaints are not expected.
  4. The behavior may initially seem strange or odd, but once understood it is acceptable. The behavior is correct, but the performance was not as comfortable as expected. Customer complaints are not expected.
  5. Good performance as expected or above. Customer complaints are not expected.
